    You might recognise this famous protozoan - an amoeba - from Biology textbooks. Although it looks fair and harmless, don't get fooled: it can be hazardous. More precisely, Entamoeba histolytica species causes a disease called amebiasis. When E. histolytica enters the human body through contaminated water, food or hands, it turns into trophozoites - a metabolically active form. Trophozoites can appear in two types:

    • Forma minuta causes asymptomatic infection and has no invasive tendencies. In some cases, it can progress to forma magna. We are targeting this form with preventive probiotics.
    • Forma magna causes intestinal infection - amebic colitis. It has an invasive tendency that leads to systemic infection. Complications occur in various parenchymal organs and sometimes leads to death. We are targeting forma magna with a rapid and specific detection tool.
