    You might recognise this famous protozoan - an amoeba - from Biology textbooks. -. Although it looks fair and harmless, don't get fooled: it can be really dangerous. More precisely, Entamoeba histolytica species causes a disease called amebiasis.
    When E. histolytica enters the human body through contaminated water, food or hands it turns into trophozoites - a metabolically active form.

    Trophozoites can appear in two types:

    Forma minuta causes asymptomatic infection and has no invasive tendencies. In some cases, it can progress to forma magna.
    Forma magna causes intestinal infection - amebic colitis. It has an invasive tendency that leads to systemic infection. Complications occur in various parenchymal organs and sometimes leads to death.

    Prevention

    About 80 - 90 percent of people infected with E. histolytica face no symptoms but can transmit the disease to other people, who might be more susceptible and face intricate complications. Such peculiarity creates favorable / conducive circumstances for worldwide spreadness of E. histolytica. What is more, forma minuta can progress to forma magna. However currently the prevention of the infection is non-specific, based on good sanitation, avoidance of contaminated food and hands. All of these reasons justify the demand for prevention tool.
